METHODOLOGY SUMMARY
======================================================================

Study Design
  Retrospective observational cohort study using IC PrecisionQ real-world
  electronic health record (EHR) data with a landmark analysis design.

Data Source
  IC PrecisionQ NSCLC dataset. Follow-up through 02/28/2026.

Study Population
  Total patients in dataset:            500
  Final analysis cohort (post-landmark): 246
    Fixed-Duration cohort:               132
    Continuation cohort:                 114

Inclusion Criteria
  - Age ≥18 years
  - Metastatic NSCLC (ICD-10: C34.X), de novo or recurrent
  - Diagnosis date: 2016-01-01 to 2025-08-31
  - Pembrolizumab in first metastatic line of therapy (with or without chemo)
  - At least one pembrolizumab infusion at ≥22 months from treatment start
  - Alive at 29-month landmark

Exclusion Criteria
  - Chemotherapy within 60 days of last pembrolizumab infusion
    (interpreted as progression-motivated treatment stop)

Treatment Gap Rule
  If two consecutive pembrolizumab infusions were >183 days apart,
  treatment was considered to have ended at the earlier infusion date.

Exposure Groups
  Fixed-Duration: last infusion 22–26 months from treatment start
  Continuation:   last infusion >26 months from treatment start

Landmark Definition
  29 months following pembrolizumab initiation.
  Only patients alive and under observation at the landmark are included.

Primary Endpoint
  Overall survival (OS): time from landmark date to death from any cause.
  Patients alive at end of follow-up censored at date of last clinical contact
  (administrative censoring at 2026-02-28).

Statistical Methods
  - Kaplan-Meier method for survival estimation
  - Log-rank test for between-group comparison (two-sided, α=0.05)
  - Log-rank p-value: 0.0076
  - Median follow-up estimated using reverse Kaplan-Meier (Schemper & Smith method)
  - Multivariable Cox proportional hazards model (deferred — see SAP)

Software
  Python (lifelines, pandas, matplotlib)
